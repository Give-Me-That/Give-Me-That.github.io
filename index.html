Abstract 摘要

Intuitive human‑robot interaction (HRI，人机交互) is crucial for enabling robots to act as assistive partners, yet existing manipulation systems remain limited. Traditional methods based on pose estimation or grasp sampling often lack stability and generalization, while recent end‑to‑end methods demand large annotated datasets and still generalize poorly. We present a lightweight system for point‑to‑grasp manipulation that builds a shape‑centric grasp database within minutes from a few demonstrations, intuitively recognizes human intent through pointing gestures, and efficiently retrieves and retargets grasp poses for execution. Real‑world experiments show high success rates on seen (100%) and unseen (92%) objects with inference latency as low as 0.148 s, demonstrating both effectiveness and real‑time capability.

Method Overview 方法概述

Stage (a): Intent Recognition — Intuitive pointing (手势指示) selects the target object and conveys grasp intent.
Stage (b): Fast Pose Retrieval — Retrieve candidate grasps from a shape‑centric database (形状中心化抓取库).
Stage (c): Grasp Retargeting & Execution — Retarget the retrieved pose to the current scene and execute safely in real time.
  • Shape‑centric grasp database — Covers 4 shape categories (27 instances), built with ~5 min collection + ~8 min processing; easily extensible.
  • Lightweight retrieval framework — Intuitive, safe, and controllable HRI (人机交互) with low latency.
  • Real‑world results — High success on seen/unseen objects; inference latency 0.148 s.
